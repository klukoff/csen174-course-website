A core part of a software engineer's job is exploring and understanding new technologies, evaluating what they can and can't do, and figuring out what's now possible that wasn't before. AI/ML APIs have opened up a huge range of new capabilities for software products, from natural language understanding to image generation to speech synthesis. But knowing what's possible requires hands-on experience, not just reading documentation. This assignment gives you that experience.

The assignment has three parts, done in sequence. Start with Part 1 in lab, use what you learned to complete Part 2, then visualize your strongest idea in Part 3. The order matters: your product ideas should grow out of your hands-on experience with the APIs, not the other way around.

## Part 1: API Exploration (Slides 1–2)

Explore at least two AI/ML APIs from different providers or capability areas. For each API, you can either run a working demo yourself (recommended) or find and study an existing example (tutorial, video, documentation walkthrough). Try different inputs. Push the boundaries of what it can do.

Some options (not exhaustive): Anthropic Claude, OpenAI (GPT, DALL-E, Whisper), Google Gemini, Hugging Face (any model), Replicate, ElevenLabs, Stability AI, Cohere, AssemblyAI.

One slide per API, covering:

- **What it is:** the API name, provider, and what capability it offers (text generation, image recognition, speech-to-text, etc.)
- **What you tried:** describe the demo you ran or the example you studied. Include screenshots, code snippets, or links.
- **What surprised you:** what did it do well? Where did it break down or produce unexpected results?
- **Limitations you noticed:** what can't it do, or what would be hard to build on top of it?

## Part 2: Product Ideas (Slides 3–5)

Based on your API exploration, propose 3 product ideas. Each idea should be grounded in a domain you know something about (a hobby, a job you've had, a community you belong to, a problem you've personally experienced) and shaped by an AI capability you explored in Part 1.

One slide per idea, covering:

- **The problem:** who has this problem, and why does it matter?
- **The AI capability:** which API or model type would power this, and what would it do?
- **How it works:** describe the core interaction in one or two sentences. What does the user do, and what does the AI return?
- **The "without AI" test:** could you build this without AI? If yes, what would be meaningfully worse? If the answer is "not much," rethink the idea.

These are sketches, not commitments. You will pitch your best idea in Week 2, and your team will ultimately choose one concept to pursue.

## Part 3: Concept Mockup (Slide 6)

Pick the one idea you find most compelling and use [Nano Banana](https://gemini.google.com/) (Gemini's image generation) to mock up the core interaction. What would the user see at the moment the AI does something interesting?

Try at least 3 different prompts and include your 2 best outputs. Keep the mockups sketchy and low-fidelity. Focus on the core interaction, not visual polish. The goal is to communicate the intriguing possibility your product offers, not to design a final UI.

Include for each mockup:

- The prompt you used
- The generated image
- One sentence on what it captures (or what it misses)
